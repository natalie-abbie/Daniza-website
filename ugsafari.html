12 Days Uganda Safari

This 12 days Uganda tour includes, visit to Ngamba Chimpanzee Island, visit to the source of the Nile for boat trips and white water rafting, chimpanzees in Kibale National park located in western Uganda, gorillas in Bwindi Impenetrable National park in south western Uganda and Lake Bunyonyi the second deepest lake in Africa and also referred to as the Switzerland of Africa.

  • Day 1: Pick up from Entebbe Airport

    On arrival at Entebbe airport, you will be welcomed by the company representative who will then transfer you to your lodge to rest. Dinner and overnight stay at Emin Pasha Hotel (luxury) Urban by City Blue (midrange) Ivory Castle Hotel (budget)

  • Day 2: Kampala City Tour

    In the morning your guide will meet you at the hotel and brief you about the whole itinerary and the entire trip. Set out for a city tour around Bulange the administrative headquarters of Buganda kingdom kabaka’s palace the official residence of the King of Buganda, Kasubi tombs where Buganda kings are buried. Have lunch and visit the Uganda Museum and return to your lodge.

  • Day 3: Transfer to Ngamba Island Chimpanzee sanctuary

    Set off for a boat at exactly 9am in the morning on Lake Victoria to Ngamba Island home to over 49 orphaned chimpanzees, reaching on time for feeding meet the chimpanzees and you can as well participate in the feeding, learn more about these chimpanzees, look out for more wild animals in Ngamba Island like monkeys, baboons, leopards, snakes, lizards and so many bird species. Return to your lodge for dinner and overnight.

  • Day 4: Transfer to Jinja

    After your morning heavy breakfast, transfer to Jinja in eastern Uganda via Mabira forest, branch off to Kayunga road and drive to Kalagala falls in Kangulumira for white water rafting. Enjoy the most popular water trip on the Nile for a full day then transfer to Jinja town for overnight. Dinner and overnight at Jinja Nile Resort (luxury) living waters Resort (midrange) Casa Mia Baliidha (budget).

  • Day 5: Boat Cruise

    Head out for a morning boat cruise starting from the source of the Nile and tour river Nile the longest river in the whole world. Touring Jinja and the source of the Nile is another way of enjoying the beauty of Uganda the pearl of Africa. Drive back to Kampala, dinner and overnight at the hotel.

  • Day 6: Transfer to Kibale National Park

    Have early morning breakfast and transfer to Kibale National park. Kibale National park is located in western Uganda and moist evergreen forest covering an area of 766 square kilometers and well known destination for chimpanzee trekking in Uganda and many bird and primate species. Kibale National park can be accessed via Mubende Fort portal road for a distance of 300kms approximately 6-7 hours drive. Dinner and overnight at primate lodge Kibale (luxury) Kibale forest camp (midrange) chimpanzee forest guest house (budget).

  • Day 7: Chimpanzee Tracking and Bigodi walk

    Drive to the park headquarters in the morning for briefing and group allocation by the ranger guides about the dos and don’ts of chimpanzee tracking. With your packed lunch and enough drinking water set off to the forest in search for the great chimpanzees guided by the rangers, follow the trails in the forest for 30 minutes to 4 hours searching for the chimpanzees, along the way watch the birds in the forests, monkeys , baboons and other wild animals. On encounter with the chimpanzees, you are allowed to 1 hour in the presence of the chimpanzees for observations, pictures, and videos for the chimpanzees and learn more about them as you head out of the forest back to your lodge. In the evening after relaxations, go for a swamp walk in the bigodi swamp sanctuary in search for other primates like L’Hoest’s, velvet monkeys, red tailed monkeys, blue tailed, Olive baboon among others. Back to your lodge for dinner and overnight.

  • Day 8: Transfer to Bwindi Impenetrable NP

    Later on after your breakfast, set off to Bwindi Impenetrable National park in south western Uganda, for a distance of 236 square kilometers approximately 5-6 hours drive Kibale National park. Bwindi impenetrable National park is home to over 500 endangered mountain gorillas and famous for gorilla trekking. Diner and overnight at Buhoma lodge (luxury) silver back lodge (midrange) Broadbill forest camp (budget)

  • Day 9: Gorilla Trekking Experience

    Enjoy your heavy breakfast and drive to the park headquarters for briefing by the ranger guides on the dos and don’ts of gorilla trekking and group allocation, with your packed lunch and enough drinking water embark on a breathtaking and memorable experience of gorilla trekking, follow the trails to the forest in search of mountain gorillas in their natural habitats. You are allowed to only 1 hour in the presence of mountain gorilla for viewing, observations, take pictures and videos and return back to the lodge. Later in the evening go for evening walk to the batwa community, enjoy the traditional dances, storytelling, handcrafts among others, back to your lodge for dinner and overnight.

  • Day 10: Set off to L.Bunyonyi

    Drive to Lake Bunyonyi in Kabale also referred to as the Switzerland of Africa and bird paradise. Relax at the lake showers as you enjoy the great scenic views and refresh from the previous fatigue after gorilla trekking. You can also go swimming as the lake is safe from bilharzia. Meals and overnight at Lake Bunyonyi Eco Resort, Bunyonyi safari Resort, Paradise Eco Hub.

  • Day 11: L.Buyonyi Canoeing

    You can go canoeing on Lake Bunyonyi and visit the 29 islands on the lake, each island has a different history to learn about for example the punishment island where young girls who got pregnant before marriage were thrown to die. Have lunch and visit the nearby forests for game viewing and bird watching. Return to lodge for dinner and overnight stay.

  • Day 12: Transfer back to Kampala

    After your breakfast, set off back to Kampala or Entebbe airport, stop over Agip motel in Mbarara for lunch and drive Entebbe airport for your flight back, en route at the equator for photography and last souvenir shopping.
